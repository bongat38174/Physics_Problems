# Task 09 – Refraction (Snell’s Law)

## Problem Statement

A light ray travels from air into glass:

$$
n_1 = 1.00
$$

$$
n_2 = 1.50
$$

Angle of incidence:

$$
\theta_1 = 30^\circ
$$

Determine the angle of refraction $\theta_2$.

---

## Theory

Snell’s Law:

$$
n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2
$$

where:

- $n_1$ = refractive index of first medium
- $n_2$ = refractive index of second medium
- $\theta_1$ = angle of incidence
- $\theta_2$ = angle of refraction

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Substitute values

$$
(1.00)\sin30^\circ = (1.50)\sin\theta_2
$$

---

### Step 2: Use known sine value

$$
\sin30^\circ = 0.5
$$

Thus:

$$
0.5 = 1.50\sin\theta_2
$$

---

### Step 3: Solve for $\sin\theta_2$

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{0.5}{1.5}
$$

---

$$
\sin\theta_2 = 0.333
$$

---

### Step 4: Determine angle

$$
\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}(0.333)
$$

---

$$
\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ
$$

---

## Interpretation

Since light enters a denser medium (glass), it slows down and bends toward the normal.

Thus, the refracted angle is smaller than the incident angle.
