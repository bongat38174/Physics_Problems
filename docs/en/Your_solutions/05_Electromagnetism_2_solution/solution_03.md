# Task 03 – Biot-Savart Law

## Problem Statement

A wire segment of length $0.1\,\mathrm{m}$ carries current $3\,\mathrm{A}$.

The point of observation is $0.2\,\mathrm{m}$ away.

The segment is perpendicular to the line joining it to the point.

Determine the magnetic field at point $P$.

---

## Theory

Biot-Savart Law for small segment:

$$
dB = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{I\,dl\,\sin\theta}{r^2}
$$

For perpendicular geometry:

$$
\sin\theta = 1
$$

Thus:

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{I\,dl}{r^2}
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Substitute values

$$
B = (10^{-7})\frac{(3)(0.1)}{(0.2)^2}
$$

---

### Step 2: Simplify

$$
B = (10^{-7})\frac{0.3}{0.04}
$$

$$
B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{T}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{T}
$$

---

## Interpretation

A small current element creates a localized magnetic field.

The field direction follows the right-hand rule.
