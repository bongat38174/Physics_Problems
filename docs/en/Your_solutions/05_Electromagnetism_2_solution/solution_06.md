# Task 06 – Electromagnetic Wave Analysis

## Problem Statement

The electric field is:

$$
E_y(x,t) = 100\sin(10^7x - \omega t)\,\mathrm{V/m}
$$

Determine:

1. Direction of propagation
2. Wavelength $\lambda$
3. Angular frequency $\omega$
4. Magnetic field equation

---

## Theory

General EM wave form:

$$
E = E_0\sin(kx-\omega t)
$$

where:

- $E_0$ = amplitude
- $k$ = wave number
- $\omega$ = angular frequency

---

### Wave number relation

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

---

### Speed of light relation

$$
c = \frac{\omega}{k}
$$

---

### Magnetic field amplitude

$$
B_0 = \frac{E_0}{c}
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Direction

Because wave is:

$$
\sin(kx-\omega t)
$$

It propagates in positive x-direction.

---

### Step 2: Wavelength

Given:

$$
k = 10^7\,\mathrm{rad/m}
$$

Thus:

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{10^7}
$$

---

$$
\lambda \approx 6.28\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm{m}
$$

---

### Step 3: Angular frequency

$$
\omega = ck
$$

---

$$
\omega = (3\times10^8)(10^7)
$$

---

$$
\omega = 3\times10^{15}\,\mathrm{rad/s}
$$

---

### Step 4: Magnetic field amplitude

$$
B_0 = \frac{100}{3\times10^8}
$$

---

$$
B_0 = 3.33\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm{T}
$$

---

### Magnetic field equation

$$
B_z(x,t)=3.33\times10^{-7}\sin(10^7x-\omega t)\,\mathrm{T}
$$

---

## Final Result

### Direction

Positive x-axis

---

### Wavelength

$$
\lambda = 6.28\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm{m}
$$

---

### Angular frequency

$$
\omega = 3\times10^{15}\,\mathrm{rad/s}
$$

---

### Magnetic field

$$
B_z(x,t)=3.33\times10^{-7}\sin(10^7x-\omega t)\,\mathrm{T}
$$

---

## Interpretation

This is a visible-light electromagnetic wave.

- Electric field oscillates in y-direction
- Magnetic field oscillates in z-direction
- Wave travels along x-direction

Thus, all three directions are mutually perpendicular.
