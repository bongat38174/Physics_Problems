# Task 01 – Gauss's Law

## Problem Statement

A point charge of $+2\,\mathrm{C}$ is located at the origin.

Determine the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius $1\,\mathrm{m}$ centered at the origin.

---

## Theory

Gauss’s Law states that the total electric flux through any closed surface is:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{Q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

where:

- $\Phi_E$ = electric flux
- $Q_{\text{enc}}$ = enclosed charge
- $\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12}\,\mathrm{C^2/(N\cdot m^2)}$

For a spherical surface centered on the charge, the radius does not affect total flux.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Identify enclosed charge

$$
Q = +2\,\mathrm{C}
$$

---

### Step 2: Apply Gauss’s Law

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.85\times10^{-12}}
$$

---

### Step 3: Calculate

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{N\cdot m^2/C}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{N\cdot m^2/C}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The electric flux depends only on the total enclosed charge.

Because the charge is positive, the flux is outward through the spherical surface.

A larger charge produces proportionally greater electric flux.
