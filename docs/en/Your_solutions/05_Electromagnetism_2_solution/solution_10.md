# Task 10 – Speed of Light in Media

## Problem Statement

Determine the speed of light in diamond, given:

$$
n = 2.42
$$

---

## Theory

Speed of light in a medium:

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

where:

- $c = 3.00\times10^8\,\mathrm{m/s}$
- $n$ = refractive index

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Substitute values

$$
v = \frac{3.00\times10^8}{2.42}
$$

---

### Step 2: Calculate

$$
v \approx 1.24\times10^8\,\mathrm{m/s}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
v \approx 1.24\times10^8\,\mathrm{m/s}
$$

---

## Interpretation

Light travels significantly slower in diamond than in vacuum because diamond has a high refractive index.

This slowing causes strong refraction and brilliance in diamonds.
