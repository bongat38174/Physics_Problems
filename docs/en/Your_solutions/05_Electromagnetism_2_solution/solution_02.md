# Task 02 – Ampere's Law

## Problem Statement

Two long parallel wires are separated by $10\,\mathrm{cm}$ and carry currents of $5\,\mathrm{A}$ in opposite directions.

Determine the magnetic field magnitude and direction at the midpoint between the wires.

---

## Theory

Magnetic field around a long straight wire:

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}
$$

where:

- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{T\cdot m/A}$
- $I$ = current
- $r$ = distance from wire

At midpoint:

$$
r = \frac{10\,\mathrm{cm}}{2} = 0.05\,\mathrm{m}
$$

Because currents are opposite, fields reinforce.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Field from one wire

$$
B_1 = \frac{(4\pi\times10^{-7})(5)}{2\pi(0.05)}
$$

---

### Step 2: Simplify

$$
B_1 = 2 \times 10^{-5}\,\mathrm{T}
$$

---

### Step 3: Total field

Since fields add:

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 2B_1
$$

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 4 \times 10^{-5}\,\mathrm{T}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
B = 4.0 \times 10^{-5}\,\mathrm{T}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The midpoint experiences enhanced magnetic field because both fields point in the same direction there.

Direction is determined using the right-hand rule.
