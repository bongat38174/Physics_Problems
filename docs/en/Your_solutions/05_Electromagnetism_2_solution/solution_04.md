# Task 04 – Magnetic Torque

## Problem Statement

A rectangular loop:

- Length = $10\,\mathrm{cm}$
- Width = $5\,\mathrm{cm}$
- Current = $2\,\mathrm{A}$

Magnetic field:

$$
B = 0.3\,\mathrm{T}
$$

Find magnetic torque.

---

## Theory

Torque on current loop:

$$
\tau = N I A B \sin\theta
$$

For one loop:

$$
N=1
$$

Since field is parallel to loop plane:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

$$
\sin90^\circ = 1
$$

Area:

$$
A = lw
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Convert dimensions

$$
l = 0.10\,\mathrm{m}
$$

$$
w = 0.05\,\mathrm{m}
$$

---

### Step 2: Area

$$
A = (0.10)(0.05)
$$

$$
A = 0.005\,\mathrm{m^2}
$$

---

### Step 3: Torque

$$
\tau = (1)(2)(0.005)(0.3)
$$

$$
\tau = 0.003\,\mathrm{N\cdot m}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
\tau = 3.0 \times 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{N\cdot m}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The magnetic field attempts to rotate the loop so its magnetic moment aligns with the field.
