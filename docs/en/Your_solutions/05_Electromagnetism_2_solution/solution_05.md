# Task 05 – Energy Stored by Charge in a Capacitor

## Problem Statement

A parallel-plate capacitor has:

- Plate area:

$$
S = 0.02\,\mathrm{m^2}
$$

- Plate separation:

$$
d = 5\,\mathrm{mm} = 0.005\,\mathrm{m}
$$

- Applied voltage:

$$
V = 500\,\mathrm{V}
$$

Determine:

1. Capacitance $C$
2. Stored energy $U$
3. Electric field intensity $E$
4. Force of attraction $F$

---

## Theory

### Capacitance of parallel-plate capacitor

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}
$$

---

### Energy stored

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

---

### Electric field

$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

---

### Force between plates

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 S
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Calculate capacitance

$$
C = (8.85\times10^{-12})\frac{0.02}{0.005}
$$

---

$$
C = (8.85\times10^{-12})(4)
$$

---

$$
C = 3.54\times10^{-11}\,\mathrm{F}
$$

---

### Step 2: Calculate stored energy

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}(3.54\times10^{-11})(500)^2
$$

---

$$
U = 0.5(3.54\times10^{-11})(250000)
$$

---

$$
U = 4.43\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm{J}
$$

---

### Step 3: Electric field intensity

$$
E = \frac{500}{0.005}
$$

---

$$
E = 1.0\times10^5\,\mathrm{V/m}
$$

---

### Step 4: Force between plates

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}(8.85\times10^{-12})(1.0\times10^5)^2(0.02)
$$

---

$$
F = 8.85\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

---

## Final Result

### Capacitance

$$
C = 3.54\times10^{-11}\,\mathrm{F}
$$

---

### Energy stored

$$
U = 4.43\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm{J}
$$

---

### Electric field

$$
E = 1.0\times10^5\,\mathrm{V/m}
$$

---

### Force

$$
F = 8.85\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

---

## Interpretation

- Larger plate area increases capacitance
- Greater voltage increases stored energy
- Small separation produces strong electric field
- Opposite charges on plates create attractive force

This demonstrates how capacitors store electrical energy through electrostatic fields.
