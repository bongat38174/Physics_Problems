# Task 02 – Combining Three 1Ω Resistors

## Problem Statement

Using exactly three resistors of:

$$
1\,\Omega
$$

Determine all unique equivalent resistances possible.

---

## Theory

Possible combinations include:

- All series
- All parallel
- Mixed series-parallel
- Mixed parallel-series

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

# Case 1: All series

$$
R = 1 + 1 + 1
$$

---

$$
R = 3\,\Omega
$$

---

# Case 2: All parallel

$$
\frac{1}{R} = 1 + 1 + 1
$$

---

$$
R = \frac{1}{3}\,\Omega
$$

---

# Case 3: Two parallel, one series

First:

$$
R_p = \frac{1\times1}{1+1}
$$

---

$$
R_p = 0.5\,\Omega
$$

Then:

$$
R = 0.5 + 1
$$

---

$$
R = 1.5\,\Omega
$$

---

# Case 4: Two series, one parallel

First:

$$
R_s = 1 + 1 = 2\,\Omega
$$

Then:

$$
R = \frac{2\times1}{2+1}
$$

---

$$
R = \frac{2}{3}\,\Omega
$$

---

## Final Result

Unique equivalent resistances:

$$
3\,\Omega
$$

$$
1.5\,\Omega
$$

$$
\frac{2}{3}\,\Omega
$$

$$
\frac{1}{3}\,\Omega
$$

---

## Interpretation

By rearranging identical resistors, multiple total resistances can be produced.

This demonstrates the flexibility of circuit design.
