# Task 07 – Capacitors in Parallel

## Problem Statement

Two capacitors:

$$
C_1 = 4\,\mu\mathrm{F}
$$

$$
C_2 = 6\,\mu\mathrm{F}
$$

Battery voltage:

$$
V = 10\,\mathrm{V}
$$

Determine:

1. Total charge stored
2. Total energy stored

---

## Theory

### Parallel capacitors

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = C_1 + C_2
$$

---

### Charge

$$
Q = CV
$$

---

### Energy

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Equivalent capacitance

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 4 + 6
$$

---

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 10\,\mu\mathrm{F}
$$

---

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 10\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm{F}
$$

---

### Step 2: Total charge

$$
Q = CV
$$

---

$$
Q = (10\times10^{-6})(10)
$$

---

$$
Q = 100\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

---

$$
Q = 100\,\mu\mathrm{C}
$$

---

### Step 3: Energy

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}(10\times10^{-6})(10)^2
$$

---

$$
U = 5\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{J}
$$

---

## Final Result

### Charge:

$$
Q = 100\,\mu\mathrm{C}
$$

---

### Energy:

$$
U = 5\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{J}
$$

---

## Interpretation

Parallel capacitors increase total capacitance, allowing more charge and energy storage.
