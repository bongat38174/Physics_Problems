# Task 05 – Kirchhoff’s Laws

## Problem Statement

Determine currents:

$$
I_1,\quad I_2,\quad I_3
$$

using Kirchhoff’s laws.

---

## Theory

### Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL)

$$
\sum I = 0
$$

---

### Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law (KVL)

$$
\sum V = 0
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Define loop equations

Left loop:

$$
4.5 - 21I_1 - 10I_2 = 0
$$

---

Right loop:

$$
9 - 11I_3 - 10I_2 = 0
$$

---

### Step 2: Junction condition

$$
I_2 = I_1 + I_3
$$

---

### Step 3: Solve system

Results:

$$
I_1 \approx 0.115\,\mathrm{A}
$$

$$
I_2 \approx 0.423\,\mathrm{A}
$$

$$
I_3 \approx 0.308\,\mathrm{A}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
I_1 = 0.115\,\mathrm{A}
$$

$$
I_2 = 0.423\,\mathrm{A}
$$

$$
I_3 = 0.308\,\mathrm{A}
$$

---

## Interpretation

Kirchhoff’s laws allow complete current analysis in multi-loop circuits.
