# Task 07 – Cyclotron Motion

## Problem Statement

An electron is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of $5000 \ \mathrm{V}$.  
It then enters a uniform magnetic field $B = 0.1 \ \mathrm{T}$ perpendicular to its velocity.

Determine the radius of the circular path.

---

## Theory

1. Energy gained by the electron:

$$
qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

2. Radius of circular motion in magnetic field:

$$
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1 — Known values

$$
q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \ \mathrm{C}
$$

$$
m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \ \mathrm{kg}
$$

$$
V = 5000 \ \mathrm{V}
$$

$$
B = 0.1 \ \mathrm{T}
$$

---

### Step 2 — Find velocity

From energy equation:

$$
qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Solve for $v$:

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}
$$

Substitute values:

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot (1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \cdot 5000}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{1.6 \times 10^{-15}}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}
\times 2}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{1.756 \times 10^{15}}
$$

$$
v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

---

### Step 3 — Find radius

$$
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
$$

Substitute values:

$$
r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(4.19 \times 10^7)}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(0.1)}
$$

---

### Step 4 — Calculate numerator

$$
(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(4.19 \times 10^7)
= 3.82 \times 10^{-23}
$$

---

### Step 5 — Calculate denominator

$$
(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(0.1)
= 1.6 \times 10^{-20}
$$

---

### Step 6 — Final calculation

$$
r = \frac{3.82 \times 10^{-23}}{1.6 \times 10^{-20}}
$$

$$
r \approx 2.39 \times 10^{-3} \ \mathrm{m}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
r \approx 2.4 \times 10^{-3} \ \mathrm{m}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The electron moves in a very small circular path (radius ≈ 2.4 mm).

The magnetic field changes the direction of motion but not the speed, resulting in circular motion.
