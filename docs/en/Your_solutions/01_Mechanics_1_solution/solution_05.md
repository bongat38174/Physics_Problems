# Task 05 – Relative Velocity

## Problem Statement

A river flows east with velocity

$$
2 \text{ m/s}
$$

A boat travels at

$$
5 \text{ m/s}
$$

relative to the water and wishes to move directly north across a river 200 m wide.

Determine the required heading angle and crossing time.

---

## Theory

The boat must cancel the eastward river velocity.

Thus the westward component of the boat velocity must equal

$$
2 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

Let $\theta$ be the angle west of north.

The horizontal component must satisfy

$$
5\sin\theta = 2
$$

Thus

$$
\sin\theta = 0.4
$$

$$
\theta \approx 23.6^\circ
$$

---

### Effective Northward Velocity

$$
v_n = 5\cos\theta
$$

$$
v_n = 5(0.917)
$$

$$
v_n \approx 4.58 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### Crossing Time

$$
t = \frac{200}{4.58}
$$

$$
t \approx 43.7 \text{ s}
$$

---

## Final Result

Required heading

$$
\theta \approx 23.6^\circ
$$

Crossing time

$$
t \approx 43.7 \text{ s}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The boat must aim **upstream** so that the river drift is canceled and the resulting velocity points directly north.
