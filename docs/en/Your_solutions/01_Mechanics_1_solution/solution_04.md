# Task 04 – Vector Calculus

## Problem Statement

The position vector of an object is

$$
\vec r(t) = (3t^2)\hat i + (5t - 8t^2)\hat j
$$

Determine the velocity and acceleration vectors.

---

## Theory

Velocity is the time derivative of position.

$$
\vec v(t) = \frac{d\vec r}{dt}
$$

Acceleration is the derivative of velocity.

$$
\vec a(t) = \frac{d\vec v}{dt}
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Velocity

Differentiate each component.

$$
\vec v(t) =
(6t)\hat i + (5 -16t)\hat j
$$

---

### Acceleration

Differentiate again.

$$
\vec a(t) =
6\hat i -16\hat j
$$

---

## Final Result

Velocity

$$
\vec v(t) = (6t)\hat i + (5-16t)\hat j
$$

Acceleration

$$
\vec a(t) = 6\hat i -16\hat j
$$

---

## Interpretation

The acceleration is constant, indicating **uniformly accelerated motion** in two dimensions.
