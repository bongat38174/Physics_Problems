# Task 07 – Elimination of Time and Acceleration Analysis

## Problem Statement

The motion is described by

$$
x(t)=2t^2
$$

$$
y(t)=3t^3
$$

Eliminate $t$ and determine velocity and acceleration.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Eliminate Parameter

From

$$
x=2t^2
$$

$$
t=\sqrt{\frac{x}{2}}
$$

Substitute into $y$

$$
y=3\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)^{3/2}
$$

---

### Velocity

$$
v_x = \frac{dx}{dt}=4t
$$

$$
v_y = \frac{dy}{dt}=9t^2
$$

Velocity vector

$$
\vec v(t)=(4t,9t^2)
$$

Speed

$$
|\vec v|=\sqrt{16t^2+81t^4}
$$

---

### Acceleration

$$
a_x=4
$$

$$
a_y=18t
$$

Acceleration vector

$$
\vec a(t)=(4,18t)
$$

Magnitude

$$
|\vec a|=\sqrt{16+324t^2}
$$

---

## Final Result

Acceleration depends on time, therefore it is **not constant**.
