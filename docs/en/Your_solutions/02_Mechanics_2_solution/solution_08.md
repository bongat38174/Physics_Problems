# ✅ **Solution 8: Work of a Variable Force**

---

### **Given:**

[
F(x) = -kx
]

---

## **1. Equation of motion and solution**

Newton’s second law:
[
m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = -kx
]

This gives the differential equation:
[
m\ddot{x} + kx = 0
]

This is **simple harmonic motion**.

---

### **Solution of the equation**

[
x(t) = A\cos(\omega t) + B\sin(\omega t)
]

where:
[
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
]

---

## **2. Work done from (0) to (x_0)**

Work is:
[
W = \int_0^{x_0} F(x),dx
]

[
W = \int_0^{x_0} (-kx),dx
]

[
W = -k \int_0^{x_0} x,dx
]

[
W = -k \left[\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_0^{x_0}
]

[
W = -\frac{1}{2}k x_0^2
]

---

## **3. Interpretation (Potential Energy)**

Work done by the force is the **negative of potential energy change**:

[
U(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2
]

So:

[
W = -\Delta U
]

---

## **4. Verify (F = -\frac{dU}{dx})**

[
U(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2
]

Differentiate:
[
\frac{dU}{dx} = kx
]

[
-\frac{dU}{dx} = -kx
]

Which matches:
[
F(x) = -kx
]

---

## **5. Graphs (description)**

* **Force (F(x) = -kx)**:

  * Straight line through origin
  * Negative slope

* **Potential energy (U(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2)**:

  * Parabola opening upward
  * Minimum at (x = 0)

---

### ✅ **Final Answers Summary**

* Equation of motion:
  [
  m\ddot{x} + kx = 0
  ]

* Work:
  [
  W = -\frac{1}{2}kx_0^2
  ]

* Potential energy:
  [
  U(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2
  ]

* Force relation:
  [
  F = -\frac{dU}{dx}
  ]
