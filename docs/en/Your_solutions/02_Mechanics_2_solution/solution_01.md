# ✅ **Solution 1: Gravitational Dependence**

### **(a) Period on the Moon**

Use the pendulum formula:

T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}

Since length (L) is constant:

[
T \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{g}}
]

On the Moon:
[
g_{\text{moon}} = \frac{g_{\text{earth}}}{6}
]

So:
[
T_{\text{moon}} = T_{\text{earth}} \sqrt{6}
]

[
T_{\text{moon}} = 4 \times \sqrt{6} \approx 4 \times 2.45 = 9.8 \text{ s}
]

### ✅ **Answer:**

[
T_{\text{moon}} \approx 9.8 \text{ s}
]

---

### **(b) Length for (T = 1) s on Earth**

From:
[
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
]

Solve for (L):

[
L = \frac{gT^2}{4\pi^2}
]

Substitute (g = 9.81), (T = 1):

[
L = \frac{9.81}{4\pi^2}
\approx \frac{9.81}{39.48}
\approx 0.248 \text{ m}
]

### ✅ **Answer:**

[
L \approx 0.25 \text{ m}
]
