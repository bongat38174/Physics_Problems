# Section 2: Mechanics II

## Problem 5: Maximum Acceleration in Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

**Question:**  
A mass oscillates in simple harmonic motion with:  

- Amplitude: \( A = 0.1\ \mathrm{m} \)  
- Period: \( T = 0.5\ \mathrm{s} \)  

Find the **maximum acceleration** of the mass.

---

### **Step 1: Understand the problem**

In simple harmonic motion (SHM):  

- The displacement \( x(t) \) of the mass from its equilibrium varies sinusoidally with time.  
- The velocity and acceleration also vary sinusoidally.  
- The **maximum acceleration** occurs at the **maximum displacement** (the amplitude \( A \)) because acceleration is proportional to displacement but in opposite direction:  

\[
a(t) = -\omega^2 x(t)
\]

Where:

- \( a(t) \) is the acceleration at time \( t \)  
- \( x(t) \) is the displacement at time \( t \)  
- \( \omega \) is the angular frequency  

So the magnitude of maximum acceleration is:

\[
a_\text{max} = \omega^2 A
\]

---

### **Step 2: Find the angular frequency**

The **angular frequency** \( \omega \) is related to the period \( T \) by:

\[
\omega = \frac{2 \pi}{T}
\]

Substitute the given period \( T = 0.5\ \mathrm{s} \):

\[
\omega = \frac{2 \pi}{0.5} = 4 \pi \approx 12.566\ \mathrm{rad/s}
\]

> **Note:** Angular frequency tells us how fast the mass oscillates in radians per second. The larger \( \omega \), the faster the motion.

---

### **Step 3: Calculate maximum acceleration**

The formula for maximum acceleration:

\[
a_\text{max} = \omega^2 A
\]

Substitute the known values:

- \( \omega = 12.566\ \mathrm{rad/s} \)  
- \( A = 0.1\ \mathrm{m} \)

\[
a_\text{max} = (12.566)^2 \times 0.1
\]

Step-by-step:

1. Square the angular frequency:

\[
12.566^2 \approx 157.91
\]

2. Multiply by the amplitude:

\[
157.91 \times 0.1 = 15.791
\]

---

### **Step 4: Present the final answer**

\[
\boxed{a_\text{max} \approx 15.79\ \mathrm{m/s^2}}
\]

---

### **Step 5: Interpretation**

- This is the **largest acceleration the mass experiences** during its motion.  
- It occurs at the **extreme points** (maximum displacement), where the mass momentarily stops before reversing direction.  
- The acceleration is directed **toward the equilibrium point**, which is why the formula has a negative sign in \( a(t) = -\omega^2 x(t) \).
