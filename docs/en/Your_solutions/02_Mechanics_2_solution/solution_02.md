# ✅ **Solution 2: Harmonic Motion**

Given:
[
x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)
]

---

### **Step 1: Identify angular frequency**

From standard SHM:
[
x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)
]

So:
[
\omega = 10\pi \ \text{rad/s}
]

---

### **Step 2: Find spring constant (k)**

Use relation:

[
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
]

genui{"math_block_widget_always_prefetch_v2":{"content":"\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}"}}

Square both sides:
[
k = m\omega^2
]

Substitute (m=10), (\omega=10\pi):

[
k = 10(10\pi)^2 = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2 = 1000\pi^2
]

[
k \approx 9870 \ \text{N/m}
]

---

### **Step 3: Total Mechanical Energy**

Formula:
[
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
]

Amplitude (A = 0.2)

[
E = \frac{1}{2}(1000\pi^2)(0.2)^2
]

[
E = \frac{1}{2}(1000\pi^2)(0.04) = 20\pi^2
]

[
E \approx 197 \ \text{J}
]

---

### ✅ **Final Answers**

* Spring constant:
  [
  k \approx 9870 \ \text{N/m}
  ]

* Total energy:
  [
  E \approx 197 \ \text{J}
  ]
