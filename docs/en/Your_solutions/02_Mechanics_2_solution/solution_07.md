# Task 06 – Energy Dissipation

## Problem Statement

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of $2.0 \ \mathrm{m}$. After each bounce, it loses 30% of its mechanical energy. Determine the height after the second bounce.

---

## Theory

> “The mechanical energy after each bounce is reduced. If $h_0$ is the initial height and $f$ is the energy retention fraction, the new height is:”

$$
h_n = f^n h_0
$$

> “Energy retention fraction: $f = 1 - 0.30 = 0.7$”

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1 — First bounce

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \cdot 2.0 = 1.4 \ \mathrm{m}
$$

### Step 2 — Second bounce

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \cdot 1.4 = 0.98 \ \mathrm{m}
$$

---

## Final Result

> “After the second bounce, the ball rises to approximately 0.98 meters.”

---

## Interpretation

> “Each bounce dissipates energy, causing progressively lower heights. This is a common real-world effect of inelastic collisions and air resistance.”
