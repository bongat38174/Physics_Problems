# Task 07 – Dynamics with Friction

## Problem Statement

A 5 kg block is placed on a 10 kg block. A horizontal force of 45 N is applied to the 10 kg block, and the 5 kg block is tied to the wall. The coefficient of kinetic friction between all moving surfaces is 0.2. Determine the acceleration of the 10 kg block.

---

## Theory

> “We use Newton's second law: $F_{\text{net}} = ma$.”

- Friction force:  

$$
f_k = \mu_k N
$$

- Normal force between 10 kg block and 5 kg block: $N = m g$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1 — Calculate friction

$$
f_k = \mu_k m_{\text{top}} g = 0.2 \cdot 5 \cdot 9.81 \approx 9.81 \ \mathrm{N}
$$

---

### Step 2 — Net force on 10 kg block

$$
F_{\text{net}} = F_{\text{applied}} - f_k = 45 - 9.81 \approx 35.19 \ \mathrm{N}
$$

---

### Step 3 — Acceleration

$$
a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{m_{\text{bottom}}} = \frac{35.19}{10} \approx 3.52 \ \mathrm{m/s^2}
$$

---

## Final Result

- Acceleration of the 10 kg block: $a \approx 3.52 \ \mathrm{m/s^2}$

---

## Interpretation

> “Friction reduces the acceleration. The tied top block resists motion, producing a friction force that opposes the applied force.”
